How Our Food Pantry Works

A dignified, self-serve shopping experience

1

Bring Your Documents

Proof of residency (must match ID) and ID for everyone in your household. Birth certificates work for babies.

2

Grab a Basket

Just like a regular grocery store, take a shopping basket and browse our shelves at your own pace.

3

Choose What You Need

Select items based on your family size. Clear signs show how many of each item you can take. Fresh produce, canned goods, and more available.

4

Bag & Go

Bag your selections just like at any store. You can visit once per week to ensure your family has what they need.

What to Bring

Required Documents (First Visit & Updates):

  • Proof of Residency - Utility bill, lease agreement, or official mail with your address
  • Photo ID - Driver's license, state ID, or passport (must match your address)
  • Household Member IDs - IDs for everyone living in your home (birth certificates acceptable for children, student IDs work for students)
Important: You can visit our food pantry once per week. This frequency ensures families have consistent access to nutritious food throughout the month. We serve Pinellas County residents.

What We Cannot Accept:

  • Home-canned goods (safety regulations)
  • Opened or expired items
  • Opened formula or baby food
We gladly accept sealed, unexpired canned goods, dry goods, fresh produce, frozen items, sealed formula, and toiletries. When in doubt, call us at 727-803-6977!
